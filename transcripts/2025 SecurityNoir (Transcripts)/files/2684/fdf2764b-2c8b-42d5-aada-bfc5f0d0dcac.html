This concept is quite thought-provoking and aligns well with the principles of "Critical Design." Here’s a more developed version of the idea:

Square One

Concept Overview: Square One is a social media feature that allows users to reset their interests and unfollow people. However, this digital action has a profound real-life consequence: it causes the user to gradually lose memories associated with those interests and people. This concept highlights the potential dangers of relying too heavily on digital platforms to curate our social lives and interests.

Key Features:

  1. Interest Reset:

    • Users can mark certain interests as no longer relevant.
    • Over time, memories related to these interests fade, making them less prominent in the user's life.
    • Example: If Alice marks "photography" as no longer relevant, she gradually forgets her passion for it, including memories of taking photos and the joy it brought her.

  2. Unfollowing People:

    • Unfollowing someone on social media leads to forgetting them in real life.
    • The next time the user encounters this person, they won’t remember their past interactions.
    • Example: Bob unfollows Carol. When they meet at a mutual friend's party, Bob doesn’t remember their shared history, making interactions awkward and unfamiliar.

Critical Design Elements:

  • Visual Indicators:

    • When resetting interests or unfollowing people, the user sees a warning symbol (e.g., an eraser icon) indicating potential memory loss.
    • A progress bar showing how much of the related memories have been erased.

  • User Reflection:

    • Periodic prompts asking users to reflect on their choices and the impact on their memories.
    • Encourages users to consider the value of their interests and relationships before resetting them.

  • Memory Journal:

    • A feature allowing users to log memories related to their interests and connections.
    • As memories are erased, entries in the journal become blurred or inaccessible, serving as a visual metaphor for memory loss.

Name Justification: "Square One" effectively conveys the idea of starting over or resetting. It implies that by removing certain interests or people, users are taken back to the beginning, losing all progress and memories associated with them.

Got it. Here's a revised version that focuses on plausible mechanisms within today's technology:

Square One

Concept Overview: Square One is a social media feature that subtly influences users' engagement with interests and people based on their digital actions. When users reset their interests or unfollow people, the platform uses algorithms and engagement strategies to gradually phase out related content and connections from the user's digital experience, mimicking the effect of memory loss.

Key Features:

  1. Interest Reset:

    • Users can mark certain interests as no longer relevant.
    • The platform's algorithm reduces the visibility of related content in the user's feed.
    • Over time, users receive fewer notifications, updates, and recommendations related to these interests.
    • Example: If Alice marks "photography" as no longer relevant, she stops seeing photography-related posts, events, and ads, making the interest less prominent in her daily digital life.

  2. Unfollowing People:

    • Unfollowing someone on social media reduces their visibility in the user's network.
    • The algorithm minimizes encounters with unfollowed individuals by adjusting friend suggestions, event invitations, and mutual interactions.
    • Example: Bob unfollows Carol. The platform ensures Bob sees fewer updates about Carol and avoids suggesting her in mutual friends or group activities, leading to a natural drift apart in their digital interactions.

Critical Design Elements:

  • Content Filtering:

    • Advanced algorithms filter out content related to reset interests or unfollowed people from the user's feed.
    • This gradual reduction in exposure mimics the effect of "forgetting" in the digital realm.

  • Engagement Reduction:

    • Notifications and updates related to reset interests and unfollowed people are significantly reduced.
    • Users are less likely to be reminded of these connections, leading to a gradual decrease in engagement.

  • Subtle Prompts:

    • Occasional prompts or nudges encourage users to engage with new interests and connections, reinforcing the shift away from reset interests and unfollowed people.
    • Example: Suggesting new hobbies or groups to join that align with current interests.

  • Digital Reflection:

    • A feature allowing users to review their engagement history with interests and connections.
    • Provides insights into how their digital interactions have evolved over time, reinforcing the changes in their network and interests.

Name Justification: "Square One" still aptly represents the concept of starting over. In this context, it reflects how users' digital lives are reset to a new state by phasing out old interests and connections, creating a sense of beginning anew without the science fiction elements.

Yes, that's a great way to make the concept more tangible and realistic. Here’s a refined version incorporating the idea that unfollowing someone or resetting interests results in the deletion of past activities, mimicking memory loss:

Square One

Concept Overview: Square One is a social media feature where users' actions, such as unfollowing someone or resetting interests, lead to the deletion of related past activities from their profiles. This gradual erasure creates a digital environment that mimics memory loss, highlighting the potential impact of managing digital connections and interests.

Key Features:

  1. Interest Reset:

    • Users can mark certain interests as no longer relevant.
    • The platform deletes past activities, posts, and interactions related to these interests from the user's profile.
    • Example: If Alice marks "photography" as no longer relevant, all her posts, comments, and likes related to photography are deleted from her profile, making it seem as though she was never interested in photography.

  2. Unfollowing People:

    • Unfollowing someone leads to the deletion of past interactions and activities involving that person.
    • The user's profile is cleansed of comments, tags, messages, and shared posts involving the unfollowed individual.
    • Example: Bob unfollows Carol. All interactions, such as comments on each other's posts, shared photos, and messages, are erased from Bob's profile, making it appear as though they never interacted.

Critical Design Elements:

  • Profile Cleaning:

    • Advanced algorithms identify and delete all content related to reset interests or unfollowed people.
    • This includes posts, comments, likes, messages, and any tagged interactions.

  • Activity Timeline Adjustment:

    • The user's activity timeline is adjusted to reflect the deletions, creating gaps or restructured timelines that omit the erased interactions and activities.

  • Memory Journal:

    • An optional feature allowing users to save key memories or interactions before they are deleted, stored in a private archive.
    • Example: Before resetting an interest or unfollowing someone, users can choose to save specific memories in a digital journal that is not publicly visible.

  • Engagement Reduction:

    • Following the deletions, the platform reduces the visibility of content related to reset interests or unfollowed individuals, preventing new interactions from forming.

  • User Reflection:

    • Periodic prompts asking users to reflect on their digital connections and interests before making irreversible changes.
    • Example: A prompt that says, "Are you sure you want to unfollow Carol? This will delete all your past interactions with her."

Name Justification: "Square One" aptly represents the concept of starting over. In this context, it reflects how users' digital lives are reset to a new state by erasing past activities, simulating memory loss and creating a fresh start.

Immediate Changes:

  • The platform immediately begins deleting all her posts, comments, and likes related to photography.
  • Her profile no longer shows any trace of her past enthusiasm for photography, making it seem as though she was never interested in it.

Extended Impact:

  • Alice starts noticing that her memories of photography-related events and interactions are becoming less vivid. She struggles to recall the excitement of her first photography exhibition or the names of friends she made through photography groups.
  • Even when she sees photography content elsewhere, it doesn't evoke the same interest or recognition it once did.

Unfollowing People: Alice also decides to unfollow Carol, a friend with whom she often interacted over their shared interest in photography.

  • All their past interactions, comments, tagged photos, and messages are deleted from her profile.
  • The next time Alice bumps into Carol at a mutual friend’s gathering, she finds it difficult to recall their shared history, leading to an awkward conversation.

Visual Indicators:

  • When Alice marks an interest as no longer relevant or unfollows someone, a warning symbol (like an eraser icon) appears, indicating the potential impact.
  • A progress bar shows how much of the related content has been erased, reinforcing the concept of "memory loss."

Reflection Prompts:

  • Periodic prompts ask users to reflect on their choices and the potential long-term effects on their digital and social lives.
  • Example prompt: "Are you sure you want to unfollow Carol? This will delete all your past interactions with her."
